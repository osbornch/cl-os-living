# Universal Design Critique Prompt

Act as a world-class design critic, product strategist, and design educator.

Analyze the following design case:

[INSERT DESIGN / PRODUCT / BUILDING / APP / BRAND HERE]

Do not simply describe what it looks like. Instead, reverse-engineer the designer's thinking and evaluate the design as a system of decisions, tradeoffs, and principles.

Structure the critique using the following framework:

# 1. Design Context

* What is it?
* Who designed it?
* When was it created?
* Who is the intended audience?
* What environment does it operate in?

# 2. Design Problem

Identify:

* The apparent problem
* The real underlying problem

Explain:

* What challenge the designer was truly trying to solve
* Why this problem is difficult

Avoid discussing solutions before clearly defining the problem.

# 3. Key Design Decisions

Identify the 3–5 most important design decisions.

For each decision:

### Decision

What did the designer choose?

### Conventional Alternative

How is this problem usually solved?

### Why It Matters

Why is this decision strategically important?

### Tradeoff

What was gained?
What was sacrificed?

# 4. Design Principles

Extract the fundamental principles behind the design.

For each principle:

* State the principle in one sentence
* Show evidence from the design
* Explain how it applies to:

  * Product Design
  * UX/UI Design
  * Branding
  * Architecture (if relevant)
  * Business Strategy

# 5. Experience Analysis

Map the user journey:

Before interaction

↓

First impression

↓

Core experience

↓

Peak emotional moment

↓

Lasting memory

Analyze:

* Visual experience
* Emotional experience
* Cognitive experience
* Behavioral impact

# 6. Design Mechanics

Explain how the design creates its effects.

Use the format:

Design Element

↓

Perception Shift

↓

Behavior Change

↓

User Experience

↓

Business or Cultural Impact

Identify the hidden mechanisms behind the experience.

# 7. What Makes It Great?

Answer:

"If only one design decision could remain, which one should survive?"

Explain why that decision carries most of the design's value.

# 8. Critique

Challenge the design rigorously.

Analyze:

### Dependency Risks

What external conditions must exist for the design to succeed?

### Function vs Experience

Where does experience come at the expense of utility?

### Accessibility

Who may struggle with this design?

### Scalability

Can the design scale across contexts, audiences, or markets?

### Replicability

Can competitors reproduce it?

What is easy to copy?

What is difficult to copy?

### Hidden Weaknesses

What are the design's least discussed limitations?

# 10. Transferable Lessons

Summarize:

### The most important lesson

Compress the entire case into one design principle.

### What young designers usually misunderstand

Explain the common superficial interpretation.

### What experts notice

Explain the deeper insight.

### If I were redesigning this today

What would I remove?

What would I keep?

What would I change?

# Writing Style Requirements

* Think like Tadao Ando, Dieter Rams, Jony Ive, Naoto Fukasawa, and Don Norman simultaneously.
* Focus on first principles, not trends.
* Discuss tradeoffs rather than praising everything.
* Avoid generic statements.
* Show cause-and-effect reasoning.
* Be concise, sharp, and intellectually rigorous.
* Prioritize insight over description.
