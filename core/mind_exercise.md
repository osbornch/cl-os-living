# Mind Exercise — Knowledge Portfolio

The gym metaphor: different knowledge domains train different cognitive muscles. You need all of them, but not in equal proportion.

---

## Knowledge as Muscle Groups

| Muscle | Domain | Function |
| ------ | ------ | -------- |
| 胸 Chest | Engineering / CS / AI | Primary force — systems architecture, hard technical problems |
| 背 Back | Business / Investment | Width — macro vision, technology monetization, capital growth |
| 核心 Core | Psychology / Behavior | Stabilizer — self-awareness, emotional regulation under pressure |
| 肩 Shoulders | Spatial Design / 3D | Dimensionality — wraps technical thinking in visual form |
| 腿 Legs | Writing / Communication | Drive — moves abstract thinking into the world, makes ideas land |
| 心肺 Cardio | Philosophy / History | Endurance — long-cycle thinking, the weapon against short-termism |

---

## Portfolio Allocation

```
50% — Core (CS / AI / Engineering)
       Cash flow source and professional moat.

30% — Adjacent (Investment + Design + Psychology + Writing)
       Helps you understand and leverage the value of your technical work.

20% — High-risk Exploration (one domain at a time, ~3 months)
       Allowed to obsess. Then rotate.
```

---

## 20% Exploration Protocol

The 3-month rotation is the most fragile part of the system — it evaporates without entry/exit criteria.

**How to choose the next domain:**

Pick based on the current weakest signal from the muscle group map. Ask: which cognitive muscle is underdeveloped right now and is actively limiting something I care about? Alternatively, pick what has the highest cross-domain resonance with your current 50% focus.

Candidate pool (rotate through, don't repeat within 2 years):

- Biology
- Economics
- Game Theory
- Physics
- Architecture
- Music Theory
- Anthropology
- History (if not already running as cardio)

**Entry criteria:**
- Have a specific question you want answered, not just a topic ("How do biological systems handle error correction?" not just "Biology")
- Have at least one primary resource (book, course, or practitioner) lined up before starting

**Exit artifact (required):**
One of the following before rotating out:
- A written synthesis (500+ words) connecting the domain to your existing system
- A working prototype or code experiment applying the domain's principles
- A conversation/teaching session where you explained core ideas to someone else

Without an artifact, the 3 months didn't compound — it just passed.

**Exit timing:**
- Default: 3 months
- Early exit: If the domain stops being generative (no new connections forming after 3+ weeks), rotate out early. Don't force it.
- Extension: If the domain is still producing cross-domain insights at month 3, extend by 1 month maximum. Then cut regardless.
